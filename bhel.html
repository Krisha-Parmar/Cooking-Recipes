Bhel

Bhel is a crunchy and tangy snack made with puffed rice, sev, and chutneys, that can be customized with your favorite toppings.

History:


Bhel, a popular Indian street food, has a fascinating history. Originating from Mumbai, it was created by combining various ingredients to create a flavorful and satisfying snack. Bhel has evolved over time, with different regions adding their own unique twists. It has become a beloved and iconic dish, enjoyed by people of all ages across India and beyond.



Ingredients:


2 cups Puffed rice

1 cup Sev

1 Onion, finely chopped

1 Tomato, finely chopped

2 Green chilies, finely chopped

1 Raw mango, peeled and finely chopped

2 tablespoons Coriander leaves, chopped

1/4 cup Peanuts, roasted

1 teaspoon Chaat masala

2 Green chilies, finely chopped

2 tablespoons Lemon juice

2 tablespoons Coriander leaves, chopped

Salt to taste

2 tablespoons Sweet tamarind chutney

2 tablespoons Spicy green chutney Garlic chutney (optional)




Recipe:


Step 1: In a large mixing bowl, add puffed rice and crush it slightly to make it easier to mix.

Step 2: Add sev, chopped onions, tomatoes, green chilies, raw mango, coriander leaves, and roasted peanuts to the bowl.

Step 3: Sprinkle chaat masala, lemon juice, and salt over the mixture. Toss everything together until well combined.

Step 4: Add sweet tamarind chutney and spicy green chutney to the bowl. Mix well to coat the ingredients evenly.

Step 5: If desired, add garlic chutney for an extra kick of flavor.

Step 6: Taste and adjust the seasoning as per your preference.

Step 7: Serve the bhel immediately, garnished with extra sev and coriander leaves.


Note: You can also add finely chopped cucumber, boiled potatoes, or any other vegetables of your choice to enhance the flavor and texture of the bhel. Enjoy this delicious and tangy snack with a refreshing beverage of your choice!
