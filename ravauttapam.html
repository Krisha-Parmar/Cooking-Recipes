Rava Uttapam

Rava uttapam is a savory pancake made with semolina, yogurt, and veggies, that can be enjoyed with chutney or sambhar.

History:


Rava Uttapam is a popular South Indian dish with a rich history. Originating from Karnataka, it is made using semolina (rava) batter mixed with curd and various vegetables. Uttapam has been enjoyed for centuries, evolving from traditional dosa recipes. Its quick cooking time and versatility have made it a beloved breakfast and snack option globally.



Ingredients:


1 cup Rava (Semolina)

1/2 cup Curd (Yogurt)

1/2 cup Water

Salt to taste

Oil or ghee for cooking

1 Onion, finely chopped

1 Tomato, finely chopped

1 Tomato, finely chopped

1 Carrot, grated

1 Capsicum, finely chopped

2 Green chilies, finely chopped

1 inch piece Ginger, grated

2 tablespoons Coriander leaves, chopped

A few Curry leaves

1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds (optional)

A pinch of Baking soda (optional)




Recipe:


Step 1: In a mixing bowl, combine Rava, Curd, and Water. Mix well and let it rest for 10 minutes to allow the Rava to absorb the moisture.

Step 2: Meanwhile, heat a small pan with some oil. Add mustard seeds (if using) and let them splutter. Add curry leaves, grated ginger, and chopped green chilies. Sauté for a minute.

Step 3: Add the sautéed mixture to the Rava batter. Mix well.

Step 4: Now, add chopped onions, tomatoes, grated carrot, and chopped capsicum to the batter. Mix everything together.

Step 5: Add salt to taste and mix well. If desired, add a pinch of baking soda and mix again.

Step 6: Heat a non-stick pan or tawa. Grease it with oil or ghee.

Step 7: Pour a ladleful of the batter onto the pan and spread it gently to form a round shape. Cook on medium heat until the bottom turns golden brown.

Step 8: Flip the uttapam and cook the other side until it turns golden brown as well. Repeat the process with the remaining batter.


Serve the Rava Uttapam hot with coconut chutney or sambar. Enjoy your delicious and quick breakfast or snack!
